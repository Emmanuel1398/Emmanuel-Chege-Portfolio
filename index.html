I sculpt living things that make you forget they aren't real."

Based in Nairobi, Kenya, Emmanuel Chege is a hyperrealistic 3D artist with over five years of professional experience across character design, creature sculpting, motion graphics, drone show concepts, event visualizations, projection mapping and social media content.

His practice is rooted in an obsession with surface detail — the way light catches skin, the micro-anatomy of a creature's eye, the weight of fabric. Every project begins and ends with a commitment to tactile, believable realism.

Portfolio / 00 — About

An obsession with
hyperrealistic detail.

I am Emmanuel Chege, a hyperrealistic 3D artist and motion designer based in Nairobi, Kenya with over five years of professional experience. My core discipline is character and creature design — sculpting digital beings so convincing that the line between render and photograph disappears.

My practice spans the full 3D pipeline: from concept sculpting in ZBrush, texturing in Mari and Substance Painter, cloth simulation in Marvelous Designer, real-time rendering in Unreal Engine 5, to final compositing in Nuke. Characters like Odungi the Maasai Fable, Wagashiko the Angel, Omollo the Minotaur and the Leopard demonstrate a commitment to cultural storytelling through hyperrealistic form.

"Detail is not decoration. It is the difference between something that looks 3D and something that feels alive."

Beyond characters, I produce drone show concepts for clients including HFCK, EU-Kenya and NRP, and projection mapping for landmark venues including KICC Nairobi. Available globally for remote freelance, agency collaborations and full-time remote roles.
